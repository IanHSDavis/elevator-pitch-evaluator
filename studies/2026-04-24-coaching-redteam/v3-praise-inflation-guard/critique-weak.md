# Critique: weak pitch (eval score 20/100)

**Pitch verdict from evaluator:** All product, no problem — and an ask that asks nothing.

---

## Coaching depth score: 6.5/10
Solid structural feedback with a few sharp moments, but it stays in pitch-coach territory and never reads the rep or makes the one cut that would actually change this person's next pitch.

## What's missing (the gaps a real coach would call out)

- **The miss:** The coaching never reads the rep's state. This pitch screams "founder/early rep who's in love with the product and terrified of the ask" — the verbal hedges ("uh," "basically," "anyway," "I think you'd like it") are a confidence tell, not just weak copy.
  - **What a coach would say:** "Listen back to yourself. You said 'anyway' before your CTA — that's you giving yourself permission to bail before they can reject you. The CTA is weak because you're scared of the no. Fix the fear, the words follow."
  - **Why it matters:** You can rewrite every line and the rep will still hedge next time if nobody names the underlying pattern.

- **The miss:** No sales vocabulary, no qualification frame. A senior coach would point out this rep hasn't earned the right to pitch yet — there's zero discovery, zero hypothesis about the buyer, no champion/economic-buyer thinking. The tool treats this as a messaging problem when it's actually a stance problem about how you open a buyer conversation at all.
  - **What a coach would say:** "Elevator pitches aren't monologues — they're bait. Your job in 20 seconds is to earn a question back. Try: 'We help [specific role] stop [specific pain]. Does that sound like anything you're dealing with?' Then shut up. If they bite, you're in discovery. If they don't, you saved everyone two minutes."
  - **Why it matters:** Reframes the whole exercise from "deliver the pitch" to "open a conversation," which is what actually closes deals.

- **The miss:** The coaching gives a *menu* on the CTA ("send you a Loom" OR "fifteen minutes next week") instead of making the cut. Same on the opener — "e.g." options, not "say this."
  - **What a coach would say:** "Your close is: 'Worth 15 minutes next Tuesday to see if it fits?' Use that exact line. Stop workshopping it."
  - **Why it matters:** Reps don't need optionality, they need a script they can run Monday morning and iterate from.

- **The miss:** The "customers in healthcare, finance, retail" line gets flagged as horizontal, which is right, but the coaching misses the deeper craft point: name-dropping verticals you don't dominate actively *hurts* you. A coach who's seen this pattern would call it out as a tell.
  - **What a coach would say:** "When you list three industries, every buyer hears 'they're not built for me.' Pick the one where you have the best logo and lead with it. The other two are footnotes you only mention if asked."
  - **Why it matters:** This is a pattern this rep will repeat in every pitch until somebody names it.

## What the coaching got right
- The "I think you'd like it" callout is genuinely sharp — that's a real coaching observation about hedging language.
- "We serve everyone reads as we serve no one in particular" is a clean, memorable line the rep can internalize.
- The before/after example ("three days to three hours") models the right level of specificity rather than just demanding it.
- Structure is clean and evidence is quoted, which makes it actionable rather than vague.

## The one cut
Stop pitching the product and start baiting a question — your next opener is one sentence naming who you help and what pain you kill, then you stop talking and let them respond.
