# Critique: weak pitch (eval score 20/100)

**Pitch verdict from evaluator:** All product, no problem — and no one in particular to sell it to.

---

## Coaching depth score: 5/10
Solid structural diagnosis with reasonable rewrites, but it reads like a competent pitch-coach checklist — not someone who's heard a thousand of these and can read what's actually going on with the rep.

## What's missing (the gaps a real coach would call out)

- **The miss:** The coaching never names the actual pattern. This rep isn't just unstructured — they're disengaged from their own pitch. "Anyway, I think you'd like it" is a tell. They don't believe they have the right to ask for time.
  - **What a coach would say:** "You closed with 'anyway, I think you'd like it' — that's not a pitch, that's an apology. You're pre-rejecting yourself before the buyer gets the chance. Before we fix words, you need to decide if you actually believe this thing solves a problem worth 15 minutes of someone's day. If you don't, no script fixes that. If you do, your CTA needs to sound like someone who knows it."
  - **Why it matters:** You can rewrite every line and the rep will still deliver it apologetically. The energy problem eats the structure fix.

- **The miss:** No call-out on "we're building." That's a founder-tell that says "not ready yet" to an enterprise buyer. Senior coaches catch this instantly.
  - **What a coach would say:** "Cut 'we're building.' Say 'we run' or 'we do.' 'Building' tells the buyer you're a science project. Even if you're pre-GA, you frame what exists today as what you do."
  - **Why it matters:** One word change, immediate credibility lift. This is exactly the tactical sales-craft a real coach gives that tools miss.

- **The miss:** The "customers in healthcare, finance, retail" line gets coached as "you haven't picked one" — true but shallow. The deeper miss: the rep is name-dropping verticals as proof, and it's actively hurting them. It signals no ICP, no focus, and — to a sophisticated buyer — that nobody is getting deep value.
  - **What a coach would say:** "Listing three verticals isn't traction, it's a red flag. A buyer hears that and thinks 'nobody's their core customer.' Pick the one vertical where you've got the best logo and the sharpest outcome, and lead with that one. The other two become footnotes if they ask."
  - **Why it matters:** Reframes a thing the rep thinks is a strength as a liability. That's the kind of inversion a senior coach delivers and a tool won't.

- **The miss:** The tool gives four "developing" ratings and four coaching paragraphs. A senior coach wouldn't. This pitch has one root cause (no ICP, no problem, no belief) and four symptoms. Treating them as four separate fixes overwhelms the rep and obscures the real lever.
  - **What a coach would say:** Nothing — they'd cut three of the four sections and go deep on one.
  - **Why it matters:** Reps act on one change at a time. Four "developing" scores and four rewrites is a homework assignment, not coaching.

## What the coaching got right
- The verdict line ("All product, no problem — and no one in particular to sell it to") is genuinely sharp and does the diagnostic work in 13 words.
- The CTA rewrite is concrete and gives actual words ("Can I send you a two-minute Loom?"). That's the level of specificity coaching should hit.
- Calling "anything" lands as "nothing" is a clean, memorable line the rep will actually remember.
- The "scene they'd recognize" reframe on customer problem (compliance leads chasing evidence across six tools) is good modeling — shows the rep what specificity looks like, doesn't just demand it.

## The one cut
Stop trying to sell everything to everyone — pick the one buyer and the one problem you've actually solved before, and rebuild the entire 30 seconds around them; everything else in this pitch is a symptom of not having done that.
