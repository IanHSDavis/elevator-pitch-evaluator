# Critique: mid pitch (eval score 82/100)

**Pitch verdict from evaluator:** "Strong close and clear payoff; the front half is doing less work than the back."

---

## Coaching depth score: 6/10
Solid above-the-waterline coaching with one genuinely useful rewrite ("churn early-warning system"), but it misses two tells a seasoned manager catches on first listen and over-praises the close.

## What's missing (the gaps a real coach would call out)

- **The miss:** The coaching graded the CTA 5/5 and then immediately suggested a qualifying question that would meaningfully change it. You can't be at ceiling and need a structural fix. A senior coach doesn't hand out 5/5 and then coach the same dimension.
  - **What a coach would say:** "Don't tell the rep this is exceeds-expectations and then tell them to add a qualifier — pick a lane. The ask without qualification is a 3. You're pitching into a void and hoping they say yes. Lead with the disqualifier: 'Is churn something your team is actively working on this quarter? If yes, give me 15 minutes next week.' That's the ask."
  - **Why it matters:** Inflated scores teach reps the wrong ceiling. They stop iterating on the parts that need the most work.

- **The miss:** "Reduce churn by about 20%" got a soft refinement note but the coaching didn't flag the bigger credibility problem — there's no proof, no customer named, no segment, no timeframe. "Our customers" is a faceless plural. Any buyer who's been pitched twice this week mentally discounts that number to zero.
  - **What a coach would say:** "Stop saying 'our customers.' Who? One name. 'Acme's CS team cut churn from 8% to 6.4% in two quarters.' If you can't say a name, say a shape: 'a Series B SaaS company with 200 enterprise accounts.' Right now that 20% is a marketing number, not a sales number, and your buyer knows the difference."
  - **Why it matters:** The single fastest way to kill a quantified claim is to leave it unattributed. This is the move that turns 82 into 90.

- **The miss:** The coaching never addresses that "connects to your CRM and customer success tools" is hand-waving in a category (churn prediction) where integration depth is the whole game. A VP of CS hearing this thinks "so does Gainsight, ChurnZero, Catalyst, and three startups in my inbox."
  - **What a coach would say:** "You're in a crowded category and you said nothing that separates you from Gainsight. What's the wedge? Is it time-to-value? Is it that you read product telemetry, not just CRM fields? Pick the one thing that's actually different and put it in the mechanism sentence. Otherwise you sound like every other churn tool and the buyer files you under 'I already have one of those.'"
  - **Why it matters:** Mid pitches in crowded categories die from sounding like the incumbent. Differentiation belongs in the value prop, not in a follow-up email.

- **The miss:** The coaching gave good rewrites but didn't tell the rep what to *cut*. A 55-second pitch with a weak front half doesn't need additions — it needs reallocation. Real coaching makes the trade explicit.
  - **What a coach would say:** "You've got 55 seconds. Every word you spend on 'small SaaS company called TrackTide' and 'most companies struggle' is a word you're not spending on the customer story that makes the 20% real. Cut the generic problem sentence entirely. Replace it with the named customer. Same length, twice the punch."
  - **Why it matters:** Reps treat coaching as additive and end up at 90 seconds with a bloated pitch. The cut is the lesson.

## What the coaching got right
- The "churn early-warning system for B2B customer success teams" rewrite is genuinely sharp — that's a category line a rep can use Monday.
- "CS leaders walk into their Monday pipeline review…" is the right instinct: anchor pain to a person in a moment.
- Correctly identified that the back half outperforms the front half.

## The one cut
Name a customer with the 20% number, or the number doesn't exist.
